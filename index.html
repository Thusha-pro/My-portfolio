About

A bit about my work and what drives it.

I'm a civil engineer and researcher specialising in sustainable construction materials, waste valorisation, and the durability of structural rehabilitation systems. My PhD at Northumbria University — funded by Northumbrian Water Group — explores how sewage sludge, a global waste challenge, can be transformed into high-strength lightweight aggregates for low-carbon construction.

My research sits at the intersection of structural engineering, materials science, and the circular economy. I've published in leading Q1 journals including Construction and Building Materials, Engineering Failure Analysis, Structures, and Bioresource Technology Reports, with work aligned to UN SDGs 9, 11, and 12.

Alongside research, I bring over four years of higher-education teaching across the UK and Sri Lanka — including module delivery at Northumbria, SLIIT, and a Kingston University London top-up programme — and three years of construction industry experience. I'm currently preparing for FHEA accreditation.

Research Focus

Four areas where I'm most active.

01 PhD Thesis · 2023–2026

Sewage sludge as lightweight aggregate for low-carbon construction.

Industry-funded research developing sintered high-strength lightweight aggregates from sewage sludge and GGBS — a circular alternative to mined materials. Combines microstructural characterisation, mechanical and durability testing, and life-cycle assessment.

02 MPhil · 2020–2023

Durability of CFRP–concrete systems.

Long-term bond degradation in acidic, alkaline, and aqueous environments, with Weibull-based reliability modelling for safer retrofitting.

03 KTP · Innovate UK 2024

Cold-formed steel channel sections.

Numerical (ABAQUS) and experimental work on web crippling of high-yield sections, in partnership with Doby Verrolec.

04 Collaborative · 2024–2025

Fibre-reinforced concrete in aggressive environments.

Mechanical and durability performance of glass and natural fibre composites under acidic exposure, with international collaborators.
